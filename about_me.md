# About Me

I’m Chase, a data analyst who enjoys working with real‑world data and turning it into clear, usable insights. I like solving practical problems, building clean workflows, and creating analytics that teams can trust.

Before moving into data, I worked in the craft beer industry and later in public works. Those roles taught me how operations function day‑to‑day, how messy data can get, and how important reliable information is when making decisions. That experience shapes how I approach analysis today: practical, organized, and focused on clarity.

I’m strongest when I’m cleaning and structuring data, troubleshooting workflows, and building processes that are easy to understand and maintain. I value clear documentation, intentional design, and analytics that feel grounded in reality rather than overly complex.

**Tools I use:** Python (pandas, numpy), SQL, Tableau, Excel, Git, and a growing set of workflow and data‑cleaning techniques.

Right now, I’m focused on building a portfolio of clean, audit‑ready projects that show how I approach real‑world data problems from start to finish.

Outside of work, I’m someone who likes intentional design, clean visuals, and learning how to make things more efficient — whether it’s a workflow, a dataset, or a personal project.
